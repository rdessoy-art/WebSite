What is an NFT? (Simple Explanation)

NFT in Plain English

NFT stands for "Non-Fungible Token" - but forget the technical jargon. Here's what it really means:

Think of it like a signed trading card or concert ticket stub:

You know how a signed photograph from your favorite athlete is special because it's unique and proves you met them? An NFT works the same way, except it's digital. It's a one-of-a-kind digital item that proves you own something special.

The "Non-Fungible" part just means it's unique and can't be replaced with something identical. Your ¬£10 note is "fungible" - you can swap it for any other ¬£10 note. But a signed photo is "non-fungible" - there's only one exactly like it, making it special and potentially valuable.

In the digital world, an NFT is stored on something called a "blockchain" (basically a secure digital record book that can't be faked). This proves that you - and only you - own that specific digital item.
